What is Formula 1?

Formula One (also known as Formula 1 or F1) is the highest class of international auto racing for single-seater racing cars sanctioned by the Fédération Internationale de l'Automobile (FIA).
A Formula One season consists of a series of races, known as Grands Prix (French for 'grand prizes' or 'great prizes'), which take place worldwide on purpose-built circuits and on closed public roads.
After each Race players receive Championship points, the better position you are in the race, the more points, if you finish 11-20 you receive no points unless you have a fastest lap where you get 1 point.
There are also points awarded to the constructors (Teams) based on their position in the race.

F1 Rules

F1 rules are quite simple but yet complicated, there are many race rules such as Race Flags and DRS.
The Race flags are "Yellow, Green, Blue, Black, Red and Checkered".
The Yellow Flag means that there is a Danger on the Track and the drivers need to go slowly and keep their delta time at a minimum.
During Yellow flag, there is a potential need for the safety car to come out in case there was a big accident.
The safety car will set the pace for the F1 cars behind before it goes back to the pit lane, and then the race can continue.
The Green Flag means that the danger has passed and the race can continue as normal.
The Blue Flag warns the driver that they are about to be overtaken by a faster vehicle (if they are lapped by them).
The Black Flag means that a driver is disqualified because of his reckless driving.
The Red Flag means that the race has been suspended eiter because of a big accident or a really bad weather contiditons.
The Checkered Flag is the flag at the end of the race where in the final lap of the race the cars are passing the finishing line.

DRS (Drag Reduced System) Rule is simple, there are certain DRS zones on the track
and if you are behind the car by less than 1 second, you can open your DRS and it will make you faster.
